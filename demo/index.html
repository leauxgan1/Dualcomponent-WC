Switch webcomponents in or out of the shadow-dom

How it works

This technique relies upon using template tags with a new attribute called shadowRootMode which can automatically attach the shadow dom while the html is parsed.

      <my-component> <!-- Uses the light dom by default -->
      <p> I am a paragraph </p>
      </my-component>

      <my-component> <!-- Uses the shadow dom and attaches it to the parent of the template -->
      <template shadowrootmode='open'>
      <p> I am a paragraph </p>
      </template>
      </my-component>

Styling

Normally all buttons with global styling look like this:

Light components (not using the shadow dom) may have their own styling provided and scoped to their custom element name, but that styling will leak out of the webcomponent

Shadowed components (using the shadow dom) may have their own styling provided which will disregard global styles and only use its own styles

Shadow and light sections can be created in the wc as well by encapsulating the template tag!
